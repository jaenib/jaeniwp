Case Study

Binz Wind

Urban design study for Zurich's Binz: softening the B2Binz wall and reopening the seam.

Binz sits between the Friesenberg garden-city and a rare IG III business zone shaped by the former clay pit. The 130m-long B2Binz office wall intensifies the edge. Binz Wind proposes a more porous interface that turns the facade into a lived, legible threshold.

  • Introduce two ground-floor passages to reconnect residential streets with the business ravine.
  • Move wet spaces to an external "active band" so the facade shows real life, not speculation.
  • Reuse the existing structure with minimal demolition: new risers mount on the facade.
  • Modular chamber system supports multiple cohabitation patterns without erasing openness.
